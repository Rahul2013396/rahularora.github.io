About me

I’m Rahul Arora, a planetary scientist passionate about understanding how worlds form, evolve, and express activity. My interests span from interior processes like volcanism and mantle chemistry to atmospheric escape, outgassing, and their combined role in shaping a planet’s climate and potential habitability.

I’m fascinated by exoplanets that challenge our imagination — tidally heated rocky worlds, sub-Neptunes with mysterious envelopes, and planets where we might one day detect volcanic activity beyond our Solar System. I love building and visualizing models that connect the deep interior to what telescopes like JWST can observe.

Outside research, I enjoy creating astronomy-themed visuals, exploring data-driven storytelling, and communicating the beauty of planetary science to a wider audience.
